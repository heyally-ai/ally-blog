Ally macOS app gets native full screen mode support

Brian Gladu December 7, 2025

Long periods of unbroken concentration that lead to deep focus and productivity are challenging to come by.

Even though we know our best work comes from focus, it can be hard to tailor our digital workspaces since they’re often designed to divert our attention. Alerts and notifications, cluttered interfacapp and document icons, and more are constantly competing for our attention.

macOS’s full-screen mode is one of best tools for giving your eyes and brain a working environment that actually helps with focus and concentration, because it hides everything but the app you’re working in.

Now Ally supports full-screen mode so you can laser focus on whatever markdown doc you’re creating or local chat you’re having.

To get started, download the latest version of the Ally AI app from the macOS app store.

With the Ally app running, click fn + f or click the green circle icon in the upper left-hand corner of the app.

Now you can chat with Ally and manage your work without getting distracted.

I’ll continue looking for new ways to leverage Apple’s ecosystem to make productivity easier. If you have an idea, let me know or submit as a feature request and I’ll add it to the community-driven roadmap.
