AI ads

Within a week, OpenAI tested what ChatGPT users interpreted as ads in its output, but someone from OpenAI later clarified weren’t actually ads, and Google announced their ads will be going live in 2026.

Many are disappointed, few are surprised. It’s widely known monthly and yearly subscriptions aren’t covering research, development, inference, and operating costs, so advertising is an obvious and logical revenue stream.

Now that AI ads are upon us, let’s talk about them. There’s something particularly unsettling about AI ads, a sense that these ads are different, and I’d like a pick apart exactly why they’re different than other ads we’ve become accustomed to. My argument is that the same advertising rules should not apply. We shouldn’t accept AI ads simply “because there’s ads every where else in our lives” and “that’s how the world works.”

What makes AI a different type of technology?

  1. Its an authority: We go to AI for answers, assistance, and guidance.
  2. It’s not media: So it shouldn’t be treated like media.
  3. Its output is highly suggestible and interference is hidden from the user: AI output can be altered at a number of levels without the user’s awareness
  4. Its an intelligence, not a media transfer: Using AI isn’t the same as watching a Youtube clip or listening to a podcast. It’s a unique response generated specifically for us, in real-time.

When we use AI, we’re getting unique, personalized content generated for us on-demand.
